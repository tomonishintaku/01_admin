Statement

I produce works using food motifs. I especially choose fast food and junk food, namely gyudon, instant noodles, and bento meals from convenience stores. These foods have been used in attempts to express today’s situation, both critically and simply, but cynically.
My major works comprise two series: the Streams of Gyudon/Streams of Instant Noodles and Convenience Store Bento Meals in a Heap. The former series represents the actual situation of modern eating habits: gyudon and noodles, which fall furiously into the public’s stomach without being tasted, are painted like natural streams. The latter depicts discarded convenience store bento meals that are seen piled up in large quantities every day, forming a heap; it expresses nuances of Japanese animism, such as finding a god in a rice grain. The “narrow-minded” external eyes of the Japanese are focused on the Japanese national flag’s proportional support. Furthermore, they are modeled after various foods, including dishes from all the countries of the world, which are depicted in a circle. This is the Japanize series. It asks for wealth redistribution as wealth is increasingly being concentrated in the hands of the prosperous worldwide. Cutting a cake into pieces symbolizes and expresses this redistribution. This is the Let's Eat, Everyone series. (May 2, 2015)
